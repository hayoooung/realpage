Intonation and Emphasis Practice

I FOUND a frog.

How to Pronounce: In this sentence, raise your intonation slightly on the word "FOUND," giving it more emphasis. This way, it shows that finding the frog is the main action of interest.

This rising intonation makes the sentence sound more engaging and helps listeners focus on the action ("FOUND").

Understanding Intonation and Emphasis

In English, intonation often rises on important words to show focus. Here, "FOUND" is emphasized to show that finding something is the key action.

Changing Emphasis: The meaning of the sentence can change depending on where we place emphasis:

  • I found a frog. — Emphasizes who found it.
  • I FOUND a frog. — Emphasizes the action of finding.
  • I found a FROG. — Emphasizes what was found.
